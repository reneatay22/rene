Municipality of Ozamis

About Ozamis City

Ozamiz is located on the east coast of Misamis Occidental, nearby the Zamboanga del Sur and Zamboanga del Norte provinces to the west, and Lanao del Norte is across the Panguil Bay.

It is bordered by Tangub to the south and Clarin to the north. Ozamiz is 52 kilometers (32 mi) from Maria Cristina Falls, the main source of hydroelectric power in Mindanao.

Transportation

Air

Ozamiz Airport, also known as Labo Airport, was reopened on July 5, 2007. The airport serves domestic flights, including routes to Manila and Cebu.

Sea

The Port of Ozamiz connects the city to Cebu, Manila, and Iligan, with local shipping lines providing transport for passengers and goods.

Land

Most transportation within the city is done by trisikad/pedicab and motorized tricycle. For interprovincial trips, Rural Transit, Super Five, UV Express, and local jeepneys are commonly used.

Culture and Festivals

Ozamiz's Charter Day anniversary, the feast of Our Lady of the Triumph of the Cross at Cotta Shrine, and the Perangat Festival are celebrated on July 16. The Feast of Our Lady of the Immaculate Conception is celebrated every December 8, accompanied by firework displays.

The Sr. Santo Nino Fluvial Parade is held every 4th Sunday of January at Panguil Bay.

Vision and Mission

Vision: A diversified international economic and agricultural hub in the country, providing its constituents better quality of life which in turn brings our nation to greater heights.

Mission: To become a self-reliant community, enjoying genuine and meaningful local autonomy that is an effective partner in the attainment of national goals.
