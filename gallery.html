Cotta Fort, Bukagan Hill & Cotta Beach

Cotta Fort (Fuerte de la Concepcion y del Triunfo)

Built in 1756, the fort was intended to repel the attacks of the Moro pirates, specifically for kidnapping people for slavery. In one battle alone, approximately 5,000 men lost their lives, according to Michael E. Fuentes, Ozamiz City spokesperson.

Historical Significance

Before Spanish colonization, the Subanens inhabited the area. The fort symbolizes strength and unity, reinforcing the aspirations of the Ozamiznons and Misamisnons for progress and solidarity.

Modern Relevance

Cotta Fort now serves as a beacon of knowledge, attracting visitors and the youth, offering a space for historical appreciation and community empowerment.

The Bells of Bukagan Hill

The four bells atop Bukagan Hill are named “St. Peter,” “St. Marien,” “St. Joseph,” and “St. Michael.” They were purchased by Bishop Patrick Cronin for Immaculate Conception Cathedral.

Due to their size, the bells could not be housed in the church, leading to their installation on Bukagan Hill through the efforts of Ozamiz City Mayor Fernando T. Bernad.

Inauguration

The inauguration took place on July 16, 1948, attended by various community figures, symbolizing peace and friendship between different communities.

Visitor Experience

  • Beautiful panoramic views of Ozamiz City.
  • Significant cultural and historical relevance.
  • Connection between various religious communities.

Cotta Beach

Cotta Beach is a small, black sand beach located in Ozamiz City, known for its beautiful sunrise views. The beach is a popular spot for local residents, who often come to swim or fish in the early morning hours.

In addition to its natural beauty, Cotta Beach is home to several historical landmarks, including a Spanish Fort, a lighthouse, and the Cotta Shrine, all of which contribute to the beach's reputation as a well-known location.

Visitors to Cotta Beach can enjoy a variety of street food options, although the area can become quite crowded, with limited space for recreational activities. It's also worth noting that the beach is located near a port, which can sometimes contribute to noise and air quality issues.
